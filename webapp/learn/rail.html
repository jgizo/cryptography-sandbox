Rail-Fence Cipher

The Rail-Fence cipher is a transposition cipher. Letters are written in a zig-zag pattern across several “rails” then read row-by-row to form the ciphertext.

Example we’ll follow:
plaintext = WEAREDISCOVEREDFLEEATONCE, rails = 3

Step 1 — Write the zig-zag

W . . . E . . . C . . . R . . . L . . . T . . . E
. E . R . D . S . O . E . E . F . E . A . O . C .
. . A . . . I . . . V . . . D . . . E . . . N . .

Step 2 — Read row-by-row

Concatenate the rows to get the ciphertext:

WECRLTEERDSOEEFEAOCAIVDEN

Step 3 — How decryption works

We can’t just write the ciphertext diagonally because we no longer know the zig-zag order. Instead, we rebuild it in two passes:

  1. Mark every diagonal position with a placeholder (*).
  2. Fill those placeholders row-by-row with the ciphertext.
3 a) Placeholder pattern
* . . . * . . . * . . . * . . . * . . . *
. * . * . * . * . * . * . * . * . * . * .
. . * . . . * . . . * . . . * . . . * . .
3 b) Filled with ciphertext
W . . . E . . . C . . . R . . . L . . . T . . . E
. E . R . D . S . O . E . E . F . E . A . O . C .
. . A . . . I . . . V . . . D . . . E . . . N . .

Finally, follow the rails diagonally (top → bottom → top …) to recover the plaintext WEAREDISCOVEREDFLEEATONCE.
