Caesar Cipher

The Caesar cipher is a classical substitution cipher, and one that is very iconic. This cipher operates by taking a letter's position in the alphabet (A=1, B=2, ...), and shifting its position by k positions.

Worked Example (shift = 3)

    Plain : HELLO WORLD
    Cipher: KHOOR ZRUOG

The Caesar cipher replaces each letter with the one k positions further down the alphabet. With k = 3:

  • H is the 8th letter (index 7 if you start at 0). Add 3 → index 10 → K.
  • E (index 4) + 3 → H
  • L (index 11) + 3 → O
  • ⋯

When the shift would “fall off” the end of the alphabet, we wrap around by taking the result mod 26 (there are 26 letters):

W → index 22  + 8 = 30 → 30 mod 26 = 4 → E
